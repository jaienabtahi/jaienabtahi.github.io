Roofing FAQs: Common Questions

How long should my roof last?

The lifespan of a roof depends on the material, quality of installation, and maintenance. Asphalt shingle roofs typically last 15-30 years, while flat roofing systems like TPO or EPDM can last 20-30+ years. Slate and tile roofs can last 50 years or much longer with proper care.

What are the signs I might need a new roof or major repairs?

Common signs include frequent leaks, missing or damaged shingles/tiles, sagging areas, granules in gutters (for shingle roofs), blistering or cracking on flat roofs, and significantly increased energy bills.

Can you repair my roof instead of replacing it?

Often, yes. We always aim for the most cost-effective long-term solution. If the roof is generally in good condition but has localized damage, repairs are often feasible. However, if the roof is old, extensively damaged, or has multiple failing areas, replacement might be more economical in the long run.

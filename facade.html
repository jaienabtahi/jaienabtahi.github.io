Façade FAQs: Your Questions Answered

What is NYC Local Law 11 (FISP)?

Local Law 11, now known as the Façade Inspection Safety Program (FISP), requires owners of buildings with more than six stories to have their exterior walls and appurtenances inspected periodically by a Qualified Exterior Wall Inspector (QEWI). LCS Restoration provides these inspections and any necessary repairs.

How often does my building's façade need to be inspected?

Under FISP, inspections are required every five years. The specific cycle (A, B, or C) depends on the last digit of your building's block number. We can help you determine your building's schedule.

What are common signs of façade problems?

Look for cracked or bulging bricks, crumbling mortar, rust stains (indicating corroding steel lintels), water stains on interior walls near windows, or loose/falling debris. Any of these warrant a professional assessment.
